The 3-Ingredient Tomato Pasta Sauce That Tastes Like It Simmered All Day

Some of the best recipes are the simplest. This tomato sauce, made famous by Italian cookbook author Marcella Hazan, is a perfect example. It uses only three basic ingredients to create a sauce so rich, sweet, and velvety that you'll swear it was a complex family secret. It's the perfect weeknight dinner hero.

What You'll Need

This is the best part. No chopping garlic, no sautéing. Just three simple things:

  • One 800g (28-ounce) can of whole peeled tomatoes (San Marzano are the best if you can find them)
  • 5 tablespoons of unsalted butter
  • One medium onion, peeled and cut in half

The Method

Step 1: Combine all three ingredients in a medium saucepan. Add a pinch of salt. That's it. Don't chop the onion, just drop the two halves in.

Step 2: Bring the sauce to a simmer over medium heat. Once it's simmering, turn the heat down to low so it bubbles away very gently.

Step 3: Let it cook, uncovered, for at least 45 minutes. Stir it occasionally, crushing the tomatoes against the side of the pot with a wooden spoon. The sauce is done when the fat from the butter has separated from the tomatoes.

Step 4: Before serving, discard the onion halves (they've already given all their delicious flavour to the sauce). Toss with your favorite pasta and enjoy. It's deceptively simple and unbelievably delicious.
